Appointment notes
Worries journal
Reassurance — common worries
Miscarriage anxiety
Very common. Risk drops significantly after a heartbeat is seen at week 6–7. After week 12, falls below 1%.
Symptoms coming and going
Totally normal. A good day doesn't mean something is wrong — your body is doing enormous work even when you can't feel it.
Light spotting
Brown or pink spotting without pain is usually implantation bleeding. Mention to midwife but not an emergency.
No bump yet
Completely normal at 5 weeks. Most first-time mums don't show until 14–20 weeks.
When to call the midwife
Usually normal — mention at next appointment
Mild nausea or vomiting
Common weeks 6–12. Try small meals, ginger, sea-bands.
Light pink or brown spotting, no pain
Often implantation bleeding — mention to midwife.
Mild cramping, fatigue, breast tenderness
Normal hormonal responses in first trimester.
Call your midwife same day
Heavy or bright red bleeding
Especially with clots — get seen today.
Can't keep fluids down for 24hrs
May be hyperemesis gravidarum — needs treatment.
Burning when weeing / high temperature
Likely UTI — common in pregnancy, needs antibiotics quickly.
Sudden loss of all symptoms
Call and ask for a reassurance scan.
Go to A&E or call 999
Severe one-sided abdominal pain
Could indicate ectopic pregnancy — medical emergency.
Heavy bleeding with severe pain
Go to A&E immediately.
Fainting, chest pain, difficulty breathing
Call 999.
